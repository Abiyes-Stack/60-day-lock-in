# The Lock-In Operating System

The goal-agnostic core. Everything here holds whether you're chasing a job, launching a product, or transforming your health. Goal-specific tooling is generated on top of this by `/lock-in-setup` — this file is what never changes.

## The thesis

A lock-in is not a grind sprint. It's a **conversion system with a deadline**: external evidence decides what you build, everything you build serves the outcome, and recovery is engineered in because 60 days at unsustainable pace produces 20 good days and 40 bad ones.

Three beliefs under everything:

1. **Evidence beats intention.** The first week gathers ≥20 scored, real-world data points (opportunities, market signals, baseline measurements). Every later investment — what to study, build, or buy — must trace back to that evidence.
2. **Proof beats claims.** The units of progress are published, defensible artifacts: a shipped project, a live offer, a measured result. Never "worked on X."
3. **Outcomes beat hours.** Success each week is hitting outcome floors, not clocking time. A 10-hour week that ships beats a 40-hour week that polishes.

## Time structure

### The 60 days

| Phase | Days | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Evidence week | 1–7 | Set up the system; gather ≥20 scored data points; first real actions (don't wait for perfect information) |
| Build sprint 1 | 1–14 | First published artifact (overlaps evidence week — the sprint starts Day 1) |
| Build sprint 2 | 15–28 | Second artifact, distinct angle |
| Build sprint 3 | 29–42 | Third artifact |
| Conversion window | 43–60 | Double down on whatever earns attention; convert, follow up, close. No fourth build without a conversion reason. |

Within each sprint: **scope days 1–2, build days 3–5, validate/publish days 6–7**, second week absorbs slip and conversion work. Not ready by the gate → cut scope and ship the smallest credible version. Never carry work past its sprint unless an active conversion needs it.

30-day variant: one sprint + conversion window. 90-day: four sprints, longer window, mandatory mid-point review.

### Daily blocks

- **One protected deep block** (60–150 min) — the day's highest-leverage work, first.
- **One themed evening/second block** — ONE preselected outcome.
- The income source and sleep are protected absolutely.
- One theme per day. Scheduling every priority every day is how nothing ships.

### Weekly rhythm

A fixed weekly template (set in the plan) assigns each day's theme: pursue/convert early week, build mid-week, follow up and recover late week, **reset on Sunday** (30–45 min: tracker, overdue items, mode choice, next anchor — `/weekly-reset` automates it).

## Operating modes

Chosen deliberately every Sunday. Floors are outcomes, defined per-goal in the plan:

| Mode | When | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Recovery** | Energy, commitments, or life disruption limit capacity | Minimum floors; three good days out of five counts as success |
| **Core** | Normal week | The standard floors |
| **Target** | Energy and opportunity both available | Elevated floors; one rehearsal/quality rep added |
| **Stretch** | Materials prepared, calendar clear | Maximum volume — never more than two consecutive weeks |

## The tracker

One file, one row per unit of work (opportunity, increment, or assessment). If it isn't in the tracker, it didn't happen. Every row carries: date, a 0–10 score with per-criterion breakdown, status in a defined flow, evidence link, and a next-action date.

**Scoring:** five criteria, 0–2 each, defined per-goal. Hard thresholds: **8–10 act now; 6–7 selective; <6 skip and don't track.** The rubric's job is saying no — prime time never goes to manufacturing fit.

## Quality bar for published artifacts

Every published artifact needs, adapted per-goal:

- One clear question/problem/claim it addresses — one, not ten.
- Sources, dates, and licenses for any data; a personally-validated core (formulas, claims, measurements). AI may draft; you must defend everything live.
- Honest labeling — simulations labeled as simulations, no fabricated metrics or testimonials.
- A distribution kit: the artifact's link, one summary line, a 60-second verbal walkthrough, two stories about it.

## Recovery protocol

When a day or week breaks:

1. Do **not** roll every missed task forward.
2. Return to the next scheduled themed block.
3. Protect sleep, the income source, and the 24-hour response SLA above all else.
4. Do the smallest action that restores momentum — one message, one rep, one 30-minute block.
5. Choose next week's mode deliberately on Sunday.

**The 24-hour SLA:** inbound interest — a recruiter, a customer, a collaborator, a booking — gets a response within 24 hours. This outranks every other rule.

## The final review

On the last day, answer from tracker evidence only:

- What converted, and what did it have in common?
- Which artifact earned attention; which was ignored?
- Did warm paths outperform cold ones, and by how much?
- Which gaps appeared repeatedly?
- What is the evidence-based next 30-day focus?

The next lane is chosen from evidence, not anxiety or novelty.
